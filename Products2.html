Youth counselors content managment system

Hanoar Haoved Vehalomed is a youth organization with numerous branches and segments. As the organization grows, a key challenge is systematically targeting and delivering content to its counselors. This content must align with the counselors' specific segments, relevant events, and other variables that influence what is perceived as valuable. Ineffective delivery could result in a decline in participant engagement, ultimately impacting income from donations and regulatory funding.

personas:

The personas can be summed into 2 segments:

1. Youth counselors – the content consumers of the site.

2. Content creators – the content creators of the site. Males and females from ages 25-50 who work in the organization.

Acute pains:

these could no longer be ignored and finally led to allocating resources for the development of the product

1. Content creators struggle to establish a centralized system for delivering content to youth counselors, leading to the loss of previously created materials and increased employee working hours.

2. Youth counselors increasingly rely on external content instead of the organization's, eroding trust and leading to a decline in volunteer participation - which in turn would see a major decline in the organizations activity

Solutions:

1. Creating a platform for content creators to upload their content, and sort it automatically to the organizations’ many segments.

2. Creating an easily accessible platform for youth counselors to access, and get pre-made content specificly tailored to their wants and needs.

KPI's met

1. More than 80% of youth counselor volunteers use the platform to consume content.

2. Eliminated work to salvage previously created content.

3. Over 600 new activities are uploaded to the site each year as content by the organization!
